Ask By Client

Frequently Asked Questions

Explore answers to your questions about autism support, educational services, and our commitment to families.

What types of support services do you offer?

We specialize in providing Behaviour Analysis (BA) therapy and Respite Services. Our BA therapy programs are tailored to promote positive behaviour changes and skill development in individuals with developmental disabilities.

How can I begin services with Essential Autism?

Fill out our contact form with your information, and our team will reach out to you promptly to schedule a consultation or meeting.

What age groups do you provide services for?

Essential Autism offers services for individuals of all ages, from early childhood through adulthood.

Are your services covered by insurance?

We accept various forms of funding, including OAP, Passport, and Respite funds, among others. We are also listed as an approved OAP provider. With Dwayne serving as our Clinical Supervisor, we can assist families in accessing OAP funding for Behaviour Analysis services. Additionally, many insurance plans cover our services. We encourage you to contact your insurance provider to confirm your specific coverage details.

What is the process for getting a consultation?

To get a consultation, simply fill out our contact form, and our team will reach out to schedule a time that works for you.

How do I know if my child needs BA therapy?

If you're noticing difficulties in communication, social interaction, or behaviour, a consultation with our specialists can help determine the need for BA therapy.

What qualifications do your therapists have?

Our therapists are highly trained and certified in Applied Behaviour Analysis, ensuring quality care for your loved ones.

Do you offer virtual services?

Yes, we provide virtual therapy sessions to accommodate families' needs and preferences.

How often will my child receive therapy?

The frequency of therapy sessions is customized to meet each individual's unique needs, as determined during the initial consultation. For families with funding, we require a minimum of 10 hours of services per week.

What should I expect during the first session?

The first session typically involves assessments, goal-setting, and creating a personalized treatment plan for your child.
